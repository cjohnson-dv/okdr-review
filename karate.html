LINEAGE

A direct line to Okinawa.

Our karate is not an interpretation. It comes through three generations of teachers from Choshin Chibana, founder of modern Shorin-Ryu, down to the floor in Waukesha.

FOUNDER · 10TH DAN HANSHI

Chōshin Chibana (1885–1969)

知花 朝信 · founder of Kobayashi Shorin-Ryu

Direct student of Anko Itosu. Coined the name "Shorin-Ryu" and established the style as a modern Okinawan karate tradition. Senior teacher to Shugoro Nakazato.

10TH DAN HANSHI · CULTURAL ASSET

Shūgorō Nakazato (1920–2016)

仲里 周五郎 · founder of Shorinkan

Senior student of Chibana. Founded the Okinawa Karate-do Shorin-Ryū Shorinkan Association in 1975. Designated a Cultural Intangible Asset by the Japanese government. Taught Kyoshi Stolsmark directly from the early years of the organization.

10TH DAN HANSHI · CURRENT HEAD

Minoru Nakazato

仲里 実 · current head of Shorinkan

Son of Shugoro Nakazato and his appointed successor. Continues to lead the worldwide Shorinkan organization from Okinawa and serves as the present teacher to Kyoshi Stolsmark.

KYOSHI · 8TH DAN

Neil Stolsmark

honbu dojo · Waukesha, Wisconsin

One of the recognized first-generation American students of Hanshi Shugoro Nakazato, and now under Hanshi Minoru Nakazato. Founder and director of Authentic Ancient Arts. Has personally promoted over 175 black belts in 40 years of teaching.

CURRICULUM

The training

Shorin-Ryu Shorinkan is built on three pillars. Students train all three from white belt forward — the depth grows, the elements never change.

Kata

型 · forms

Traditional solo forms preserving the techniques and tactics handed down from Okinawa. The library of the art — everything else is contained within them.

  • Fukyu-gata
  • Pinan series
  • Naihanchi series
  • Passai, Kusanku, Chinto
  • Gojushiho and advanced kata

Kumite

組手 · partner training

Structured partner drills that bridge kata practice and live application. From single-step exchanges to flowing two-person sets, building timing, distance, and control.

  • Ippon kumite (one-step)
  • Yakusoku kumite (prearranged)
  • Jiyu kumite (free practice)
  • Bunkai application drills

Bunkai

分解 · analysis

The hidden meaning of kata — extracting practical self-defense applications from the forms. This is where centuries of Okinawan technique reveal themselves to the serious student.

  • Striking and blocking applications
  • Joint locks and throws
  • Self-defense scenarios
  • Traditional Okinawan body mechanics
